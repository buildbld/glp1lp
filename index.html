GLP-1 drugs do one thing.
Diet does everything else.

The drug suppresses appetite. Without a dietitian building your nutrition around that, the results won't last.

You eat less. But are you eating right?

Reduced appetite means reduced intake of protein, iron, and key vitamins. That shows up as fatigue, hair loss, and skin changes. Doctors are calling it the "Mounjaro face."

Weight loss without structure doesn't last

Studies show most people regain two thirds of their lost weight within one year of stopping GLP-1 medication. The drug was doing the work, not the habits.

The drug creates a window. Nutrition keeps it open.

The months on GLP-1 are when dietary habits must be built. That is the only thing that protects results long term. That is exactly what Niwi does.
